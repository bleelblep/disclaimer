03 prior callsigns

what it was called before

three names, same drawer. each one a costume change — mascot first, then palette, then the palette won.

  1. 01 glyphbuddy retired

    glyphbuddy, first. a catch-all utility — every glyph idea piled into one app before i knew which ones were keepers. it got crowded fast.

  2. 02 glupo retired

    then glupo. i built the ufo abductor, fell for the cows and the beam, and leaned the whole app into alien kitsch. around the same time the 1950s palette landed — amber, chartreuse, coral, aqua — cream grounds, brown line-work, a whole mid-century mood.

    after a while the aesthetic had outgrown the mascot. saucers were saying one thing; the palette and the type were saying another. so i gutted the ui and rebuilt around what the colors had been gesturing at all along — atomic age, orbital rings, starbursts, populuxe.

  3. 03 glypnik current

    glypnik. glyph + -nik, the slavic suffix for "one who is of" — the h quietly dropped somewhere over the baltic. sputnik (1957) was "fellow traveler" — спутник, one who travels the path alongside. this one travels the glyph.

04 catalog entry
file · glypnik-4.03 private collection

glypnik is the drawer's current callsign — a junk drawer for every glyph idea that didn't warrant its own app, and a few that eventually did.

it isn't listed, isn't signed, isn't supported. if a toy gets good enough, it moves out and gets its own page. everything else just hangs around keeping the wiring warm.

as of v2.0 the app finally dressed up to match these pages — bingsu palette, stamped controls, orbital watermarks, a uniform 4×2 cabinet. the website was a sketch for the app; the app caught up.
